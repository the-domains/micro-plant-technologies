Human populations are increasing and so is uncertainty regarding food and water supplies. Since all of our food is initially sourced by photosynthetic primary producers, we need to discover those producers that can grow fastest and that can grow in the widest variety of environmental conditions. With this knowledge, we will be able to supply the needs of the growing human population.

Micro-plant-tech is the way to do this. By utilising the core strengths of photosynthetic microbial primary producers, we will be able to produce food, fuel, cosmetics and medicines to meet the growing need of the developing world.
